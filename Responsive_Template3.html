LAVCA and EY published the third edition of The Latin American Private Equity Deal Book & ESG Cases, highlighting 11 investments ranging across stages, countries, and sectors. Deals include consumer/retail, agribusiness, services, education, oil & gas, and more.

Access the latest deal book, as well as past editions, totaling over 35 deals here.

In the coming weeks, an independent panel of LPs will evaluate these cases and others for the Latin American Private Equity Awards.

Awards will be announced during the Latin American panel and luncheon on March 7 at the PEI-UNPRI Responsible Investment Forum in NY.
 
 

In late August LAVCA published data on PE/VC activity in Latin America for the first half of 2016. Between elections, impeachments, and peace agreements, it has been an eventful year in the region’s major markets, at the same time that economies are struggling to return to growth in an environment of weak demand for commodities. So the data gave us a great opportunity to identify trends amidst the turbulence.

This briefing explores insights from investors across Latin America and around the world in terms of the immediate and longer-term impact of the Trump presidency on the investment environment in the region.

LAVCA is in the final stretch of collecting data for the annual survey on PE/VC activity in Latin America. Aggregate findings will be featured in 2017 LAVCA Industry Data and Analysis and stats are regularly cited in global and Latin American media (The Wall Street Journal, Financial Times, New York Times, etc.)

200 fund managers participate in LAVCA data collection, which is a key element to promoting capital flows into the region and helps to educate investors about the private equity and venture capital industry in Latin America.

If you are a fund manager active in the region and would like to participate in the survey, please contact Natalia Valencia or Eduardo Roman.

 
 

In November 2016, professionals from the private equity industry participated in the LAVCA/Wharton Latin American Private Equity Professional Development Program, hosted in Miami.

Vice Presidents, Principals, and Directors at PE firms and family offices, as well as institutional investors from the US, Europe, and Latin America participated in 20 sessions facilitated by Wharton and industry practitioner faculty.

Congratulations to those that received accreditation, including Aqua Capital, Arzentia Capital, Auria Capital, CPPIB, DEG, EFM Capital, Fondo de Fondos, Geribá Investimentos, IFC, Inversiones Tamer, Kandeo Investment Advisors, Linzor Capital Partners, Macrocapitales, Peninsula Participações, Puerto Asis, Stratus Group, The Abraaj Group, UTIMCO, and others.

Applications for the 2017 program (November 13-16), are being accepted.
